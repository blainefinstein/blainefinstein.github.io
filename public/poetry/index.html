Blaine Finstein

12/24/2019

In the midst of a typhoon, the borders between natural phenomena collapse. The sea turns along its axis and roars into a new dimension. Wind in land in sea in sky. Everything goes back to the point of origin; everything goes back to the water.

//

Some tragedies do not come with a warning. Sometimes there is no transition. Silence turns into screaming without a wail, without a sniffle or a dull cry. One moment you’re breaking bread on the front porch and the next you’re seeing through water.

//

water on the sheets water up to my ankles water falling from the ceiling water coming in through the gap in the window water filling up the sink water soaking through the edge of my passport water in the cabinet when I leave it open for too long waterlogged book waterlogged card from home water rushing to fill every open space

Splish Splash The world goes topsy turvy.

//

In the midst of a typhoon, I drift into a dreamless half-sleep. Awake when the wind starts to scream into my ear. I go to the kitchen because I do not know what else to do with my body, trapped in the house like cornered prey with nowhere to hide. I pace through the water while I ponder the logic of the event. How it is that it is windy inside my house. How it is that the storm rips trees from the ground like they are weeds, flicks them around like an afterthought. How it is that my Euclidean geometry has been deconstructed, the world outside my window a mass of shapes, a pile of equations following the wrong rules.

Ate comes in, fumbling with a lighter. “Are you scared, anak? I lit a candle. This will all be over in the morning.”

“I’m not scared,” I reply, when what I mean to say is “how could I not be?” I am stretched across the world. The street is filling up with water and I cannot remember if I know how to swim.

//

It is not a mother’s job to fix your mistakes, and you are mine. My ungrateful progeny. My fallen legacy. I have lost patience for your insolence. You treat me as though I will always be this silent. You forget my name - I who am your blood - I who gave you yours. I will snap you from limb to limb. I will break your spirit until you understand what you have done to me. I will drown your homes and leave you with nothing. You will sleep in a puddle of your own filth. You will not recover for weeks. This one was a warning, child. Heed my cry.

//

Morning carries the sunrise into the sky like a long forgotten promise. Terrifying, this new light. What world will it uncover? What is left for us now?

The answer is a neighborhood, a wreckage, that I cannot recognize, a geography unwritten. All of the almanacs turned to filth.

Scraps of my life lay strewn about, torn to jetsam between the borders of night and day as they cleaved open for me this new land - nothing but earth.

//

Squatting in the bamboo scraps of a former house, my friend says to me, with a laugh, I might add -

“We’re refugees now. What a life.”

We open gifts in the floodwater, the door to our house open, welcoming a constant shuffle of neighbors, as it always is. Chyme smiles and makes ripples with the tip of her index finger as it breaks the surface of the water.

“Masadya ang bagyo,” laughs kuya, his tone uninflected.

“Masadya pa gid,” I retort, mine still waterlogged and floating a hundred kilometers away.

//

I know that you are crying now, amidst all of this destruction, but remember that you are a guest on this earth. The land is just the region of shore that I have been gracious enough to recede from. Do not forget that I am the one that gave you life. I made space for you in the world. This land is my own and I nurture you from it, child. Who will you be outside of it? There’s nowhere to run. I’ll turn the beach into a noose and tighten the rope until you’re hanging from the sky. Feel the weight of your choices upon your neck, child. Ain’t that shit heavy?

//

“We will rise, anak. Don’t be scared. We will rise.”
